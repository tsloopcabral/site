Ph.D Research

My Ph.D research was focused on the dynamic compression and tensile failure of additively manufactured (AM) 316L SS with controlled porosity, which essentially means that I shot a cannon at steel with holes in it to see how it fails.

My findings revealed that the presence of these pre-existing pores reduced void formation and visible damage. However, the extent of this effect was highly dependent on pore size and configuration, highlighting the complex relationship between porosity and material strength.
